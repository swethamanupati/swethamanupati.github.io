Urban Mobility / Retention

Citi Bike Usage & Customer Behavior

Analyzed ~16,800 Citi Bike trips in NYC to uncover usage patterns across age groups, commuting schedules, and subscriber segments, informing decisions on bike deployment and targeted marketing.

  • Insight: ~98% of trips are driven by recurring subscribers who exhibit predictable 8 AM and 5-6 PM commute peaks.
  • Insight: High-demand geographical hotspots cluster tightly around transit hubs (e.g., Grove St PATH).
  • Recommendation: Increase rebalancing frequency at key transit hubs and launch "guaranteed bike" loyalty perks for core 25-45 age segments.

Healthcare / Cost Drivers

Health Insurance Claims Utilization

Analyzed de-identified medical claims data to identify service categories and member segments driving costs, focusing on potentially avoidable high-cost utilization like non-emergent ER visits.

  • Insight: A small share of high-risk members drives the majority of medical spend, heavily concentrated in inpatient/ER services.
  • Insight: Pharmacy spend is heavily isolated to a few chronic-condition drug classes with varied adherence.
  • Recommendation: Launch proactive care-management and telehealth alternatives for high-risk cohorts to reduce avoidable ER visits.

Finance / Churn Risk

Retail Banking Customer Profitability

Segmented retail banking customers using transaction, product mix, and behavioral data to determine profitability indices and model early warning signals for churn risk.

  • Insight: Customers with "relationship bundles" (checking + savings + credit) generate higher monthly profit and dramatically lower churn.
  • Insight: A reduction in digital logins and card transactions over a 3-month rolling period serves as a highly accurate churn precursor.
  • Recommendation: Nudge single-product customers into relationship bundles using targeted in-app messaging prior to engagement drop-offs.
